About Me

I'm a filmmaker driven by the belief that stories can transform how we see the world. With a background spanning documentary, narrative, and experimental film, I've dedicated my career to amplifying voices that often go unheard.

After graduating from NYU's Tisch School of the Arts in 2018, I've worked on projects that have screened at Sundance, SXSW, and Tribeca. My work explores themes of identity, social justice, and human connection.

My Journey

2018

Graduated from NYU Tisch School of the Arts with honors. Directed thesis film "Echoes of Silence" which won the student showcase award.

2019-2020

Assistant Director on "The Crossing," which premiered at Sundance. Directed my first documentary short "Voices Unheard" exploring immigrant experiences.

2021-2022

Received the Emerging Filmmaker Grant. Produced and directed "Beyond Borders," which screened at SXSW and won audience choice award.

2023-Present

Working on my first feature film "The Silent Revolution" while teaching film workshops for underserved youth in my community.

Why I Create

I believe in the transformative power of cinema. Film has the unique ability to transport us into different realities, to help us understand experiences beyond our own, and to inspire action.

My mission as a filmmaker is to create work that challenges perspectives, sparks meaningful conversations, and ultimately contributes to positive social change.

"I don't just want to tell stories—I want to tell stories that matter, stories that might change how someone sees the world, even if just for a moment."

Vision

To create films that bridge divides and foster empathy across different communities and experiences.

Passion

Driven by a deep love for visual storytelling and its power to connect us to our shared humanity.

Purpose

To amplify underrepresented voices and explore stories that challenge the status quo.
